About

I am actively looking for a PhD position starting from Fall 2025.

I am currently an AI research resident at FPT AI Center advised by Prof. Dung D. Le at VinUni. During my undergraduate years, I was advised by Prof. Tho Quan and also collaborated closely with Prof. Thai Le at Indiana University Bloomington.

My primary research interests span across natural language processing (NLP), reinforcement learning (RL), graph neural networks (GNN), and machine learning (ML). My long-term goals are to use ML techniques to advance the robustness, fairness, explainability, and efficiency of NLP systems (e.g. Machine Translation, Question Answering and Text Classification), Reinforcement Learning applications (e.g. Autonomous Driving, Traffic Control Systems, and Complex Social Systems), Graph Neural Network applications (e.g. Drug Discovery, Traffic Prediction, Social Network Analysis, Recommender Systems). I also hold a strong interest in problems of Generative AI, such as Hallucination, Watermarking, and Jailbreaking, etc., to ensure their safe, robust and effective utilization in real-world scenarios. My most recent research centers on i) elucidating factors affecting the robustness of NLP models, and ii) developing effective and efficient tools for forecasting the robustnes of NLP models.
