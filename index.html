About

I am a research resident at FPT AI Center advised by Prof. Dung D. Le. During my undergraduate years, I also closely collaborated with Prof. Thai Le at University of Mississippi (Ole Miss) and Dr. Hoang Tran at Oak Ridge Lab.

My primary research interests span across natural language processing (NLP), reinforcement learning (RL), graph neural networks (GNN), and machine learning (ML). My long-term goals are to use ML techniques to advance the robustness, reliability, privacy, and fairness of NLP systems (e.g. Machine Translation, Question Answering and Text Classification), Reinforcement Learning applications (e.g. Autonomous Driving in Adverse Weather Conditions, Traffic Control Systems, and Complex Social Systems), Graph Neural Network applications (e.g. Drug Discovery, Traffic Prediction, Social Network Analysis, Recommender Systems). I also hold a strong interest in problems of Generative AI, such as Hallucination, Watermarking, and Jailbreaking, etc., to ensure their effective utilization in real-world scenarios. My ongoing research centers on i) elucidating factors affecting the robustness of NLP models, and ii) developing fast and efficient tools for forecasting the robustnes of NLP models. Here are some keywords:

  • Fair AI (FAI)
  • Explainable AI (XAI)
  • Generative AI (GAI)
  • Adversarial Attack/Robustness, Robust AI (RAI)
